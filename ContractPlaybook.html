Contract Playbook

Overview

The organization aimed to establish a system enabling CM/CLs to effortlessly identify and engage in negotiations with suppliers they are collaborating with.

Products of Focus

  • Contract Clauses
  • Alternative clause options and comparisons to the standard clauses
  • Capability to review any legal team recommendations or considerations.

Context

  • Role — UX/UI Designer
  • Tools— Figma, Miro, Google Slides
  • Timeline — October 2022 to April 2023 (Phase 1)

The Process

The Problem

I was brought on board to tackle the challenges associated with the existing Contract Playbook tool within procurement. These challenges included users' difficulty in finding necessary information due to feature overload, leading to frustration. Moreover, the tool suffered from inconsistent branding across the product suite, contributing to a disjointed user experience. Additionally, its poor performance and recurring bugs hampered its functionality.

The Goal

My aim was to comprehend the stakeholders' objectives for the tool and enhance the overall workflow, with the overarching goal of restoring confidence in the contracting solution for CM/CLs.

Understanding

The duration and the activities involved in my process may fluctuate depending on when I become a part of a project, the available resources, and the project's development deadline. In the context of this project, we will delve into my process as illustrated in the diagram below.

Discovery

The Discovery phase is the process of collecting and analyzing information about the project, its intended market, and its audience.

Getting Up-to-Speed

Before suggesting any potential solutions, it was imperative to gain a thorough understanding of the current product, the market, the industry, and the end-users. I had to acquire comprehensive knowledge about the industry and the product in its current state.

What was done

  • 1-on-1 Sessions with PM and SME's
  • Product Demos of existing solutions
  • Requirements Gathering
  • Competitive Analysis
  • Market Research
  • Customer Interviews
  • User Personas
    • Personas and Customer Interviews

    Focusing on our procurement teams, our primary audience primarily consists of Category Managers and Category Leaders (CM/CLs). We established personas representing these professionals and consistently consulted them throughout the entire design process.

    Interviews

    I set up interviews with 10 of our end users to learn their process, which tools they use daily, what they like, their needs, and their pain points.

    Key Findings
    • A significant number of our customers have developed individualized solutions to address existing usability challenges.
    • Different teams had unique requirements and anticipated the tool to be flexible in accommodating these needs.
    • Within the same team, managers employed the software in diverse ways.
    • All customers expressed a preference for enhancements that could enhance the application's quality and were enthusiastic about fresh possibilities.
    • Users needed the capability to access the legally approved clause and key considerations while also having the option to explore potential alternative clauses.
    Analyzing and Reviewing the Existing Product

    Comprehending the current product was vital in determining which solutions to recommend. I established daily 1 on 1 sessions with the Team Lead and SMEs, during which we conducted in-depth walkthroughs of the application to gain insight into its core features and functionality.

    In the initial phases, I identified numerous usability issues that I classified as "low-hanging fruit." These issues were readily identifiable and could be addressed by adhering to fundamental usability standards without causing any disruption to the existing user experience.

    Competitive Analysis

    Before outlining the steps for a proposed solution, I will dedicate time to conducting thorough research and analysis of our competitors.

    The objective of this competitive analysis is to assess the strengths and weaknesses of our competitors' solutions within the market, with a focus on identifying areas for improvement and comparison.

    Key observations from the analysis are as follows:

    • Contract Playbook excels in overall functionality but falls short in terms of UX execution.
    • CP lags in terms of basic usability and functionality when compared to competitors.
    • Competing solutions outperform CP in terms of UX design, usability, and user interaction.
    • Competing solutions demonstrate superior performance and speed while delivering an overall smoother user experience.

Before finalizing a visual direction, I typically explore my preferred sources for inspiration. Websites like Dribbble, Pinterest, or Google Images serve as excellent platforms for staying informed about the latest design trends and gathering fresh concepts for potential visual solutions.

Design & Rapid Prototyping

Rapid prototyping is the process of producing interactive prototypes quickly and early to obtain feedback. Using initial task flows, sketches, and wireframes, I’ll produce interactive prototypes using tools such as InVision or Figma.

  • Designing initial screens and interactions
  • Figma
  • Interactive prototypes

Evaluate

I will regularly present interactive prototypes to the Team Lead and essential Stakeholders to obtain early insights and feedback. After several rounds of team revisions, we will present our concepts to customers to gather their input.

In this project, I conducted user demonstrations involving 3 users, including managers, legal and administrators from procurement. By incorporating user perspectives and feedback, I made necessary design adjustments in preparation for the finalization of the project.

The process involved customer interviews, demonstrations, and feedback sessions conducted via GChat, with an emphasis on frequent iterations and comprehensive data collection.

Finalize Visual Designs

Following extensive discussions and iterative cycles involving Team Lead, SMEs, stakeholders, and end-users, I will create the ultimate designs and screens for approval, preparing for the development phase.

The process involves:

  • Crafting Visual UI Designs in High-Fidelity.
  • Utilizing company Design System in Figma.
  • Maintaining clear communication across teams.
  • Obtaining final sign-offs.
  • Delivering all required assets to teams by attaching files to corresponding stories.

Ship, Monitor and Learn

The job isn’t done unless we ship. I make it a priority to work with the Product Development org closely to get releases out to the market on time. This involves working within sprints and supporting scrum teams, cutting back functionality, conducting QA testing, and constantly communicating with engineers throughout the dev process.

Future State

The Contract Playbook serves as an interim solution towards developing a comprehensive contract builder tool. The next phase of this project involves integrating AI technology to automate the drafting of major clauses and sections. This advancement will afford users additional time to focus on critical aspects of the procurement process.
